HR Policy · 01

Attendance & Working Hours

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR
Applies To
All Employees

1. Purpose

This policy outlines Innovfix Private Limited's approach to attendance and working hours. We operate on a foundation of trust, ownership, and flexibility — believing that great work is about output and accountability, not clock-watching.

2. Standard Working Hours

The reference working hours at Innovfix are 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday to Friday. Saturday and Sunday are official off days for all employees.

These hours serve as a guideline for team collaboration and availability — not as a rigid login/logout requirement. Employees are expected to be reachable and productive during core business hours, ensuring smooth communication with teammates and stakeholders.

3. Flexible Attendance

There is no mandated login time. Employees are trusted to manage their schedules responsibly, provided they:

  • Complete their assigned work and meet deadlines
  • Are available for team meetings and collaboration windows
  • Communicate proactively with their manager if their availability differs on a given day

4. Work Model

Work arrangements at Innovfix are role-dependent. The applicable model (in-office, hybrid, or remote) for each role will be communicated at the time of joining or as updated by the respective manager and HR. Employees are expected to adhere to the arrangement applicable to their role.

5. Attendance Tracking

Employees are responsible for self-reporting their attendance on Tessa on a daily basis. This includes marking presence, work-from-home days, or leave. Accurate and timely self-reporting is a professional expectation at Innovfix.

If an employee needs to step out or leave early for 1–2 hours during the working day, they must apply for a Permission on Tessa before stepping out or, at the latest, by end of day.

6. Leave & Attendance Options on Tessa

All leave and attendance updates must be logged on Tessa. The portal covers the following categories:

Leave Type Description Approval
Casual Leave For planned personal needs. No pay cut applies. Manager Approval
Sick Leave For health-related absences. Auto-Approved
Emergency Leave For situations requiring immediate unplanned time off, under sick or casual circumstances. Auto-Approved
Menstrual Leave Available to employees as needed, no questions asked. Auto-Approved
Work From Home For days where an employee works remotely. Manager Approval
Permission For short absences of 1–2 hours during the working day. Manager Approval
Compensatory Off For employees who work on a weekend at their manager's direction. Manager Approval
The company's public holiday calendar is available on Tessa. Public holidays are separate from leave entitlements. Whatever the nature of the absence or adjustment, Tessa is the single source of truth. Employees are expected to keep their records updated at all times.

7. Weekend Work

Saturday and Sunday are official off days at Innovfix. In exceptional cases, if an employee is required to work over the weekend, this must be at the explicit direction of their manager. Such employees are entitled to either a Compensatory Off or additional incentive compensation, as determined by the manager.

8. Unplanned Absences

If an employee is unable to come in or work on a given day without prior planning, they must inform their direct manager on the same day and log the absence on Tessa at the earliest opportunity. Prompt communication is expected as a matter of professional courtesy.

9. Sick & Emergency Leave

Innovfix offers unlimited sick and emergency leave with no loss of pay. If an employee is unwell or facing an emergency, they are required to mark the appropriate leave on Tessa — it will be auto-approved instantly.

For absences extending beyond 3 consecutive days, employees may be required to submit a medical certificate upon return.

Regardless of the leave, employees are expected to take full accountability for their work. They must ensure that pending tasks are either completed or handed over to a colleague who can cover, so that no work is left unattended or delayed due to their absence.

10. Probation Period

Employees serving their probation period are not entitled to any leave. Any absence during probation must be communicated to the direct manager and HR immediately, and will be treated as leave without pay unless otherwise decided by management.

11. Responsibilities

Employees
  • Self-report attendance honestly and consistently on Tessa
  • Apply for the correct leave type on Tessa in a timely manner
  • Ensure their work is covered or completed during any absence
  • Deliver on their commitments regardless of when or where they work
Managers
  • Review and act on leave requests on Tessa promptly
  • Ensure their team's attendance records are accurate
  • Flag patterns of concern to HR without delay
  • Model the behaviour expected of their teams
Non-Compliance: Consistent failure to self-report attendance or log leave accurately on Tessa will be flagged and may be treated as a disciplinary matter.

12. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR or earlier if organisational needs change.

HR Policy · 02

Performance Management

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR
Applies To
All Employees

1. Purpose

This policy establishes a clear and structured framework for evaluating, recognising, and improving employee performance at Innovfix Private Limited. It is designed to foster a culture of accountability, continuous growth, and merit-based reward — ensuring that every individual's contribution is assessed fairly and transparently.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all full-time employees of Innovfix Private Limited. Probationary employees, contract staff, and interns may be subject to a modified review process as communicated by HR at the time of joining.

3. Performance Review Cycle

Innovfix follows a performance review cycle that is individual to each employee based on their date of joining.

  • Mid-Year Review (6 Months): Conducted exclusively for employees who have demonstrated exceptional performance during their first six months. The decision to conduct a mid-year review is at the discretion of Senior Management and higher authorities.
  • Annual Review (12 Months): The standard review for all employees, conducted at the completion of 12 months from their date of joining. Subsequent reviews continue every 12 months thereafter.
HR will track and communicate each employee's review schedule individually and ensure both the employee and their manager are notified well in advance.

4. Goal Setting

At Innovfix, goal setting is entirely KPI-based. Each employee is assigned a defined set of Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) relevant to their role and department at the beginning of every review cycle.

KPIs are set by the reporting manager in consultation with the department head and communicated to the employee within the first two weeks of each cycle. These KPIs serve as the primary benchmark against which performance will be measured throughout the cycle.

Employees are expected to:

  • Understand their KPIs clearly before the cycle begins
  • Track and update their KPI progress regularly
  • Raise any concerns regarding their KPIs with their manager at the earliest
Any revision to KPIs mid-cycle must be approved by the department head and communicated to the employee formally.

5. Performance Evaluation

Performance is evaluated entirely on the basis of KPI achievement, overall contribution, and conduct observed during the review cycle. The evaluation is carried out by the reporting manager, reviewed by the department head, and finalised by Senior Management and higher authorities.

For employees in lead or managerial positions, the performance of their team and their effectiveness as a people manager is also taken into consideration during the evaluation.

6. Performance Rating

The performance of every employee is assessed and rated by their reporting manager, reviewed by the department head, and finalised by Senior Management and higher authorities.

Ratings are based on overall KPI achievement, contribution, and conduct observed during the cycle. No rating is communicated to the employee until it has been formally approved by the higher authorities.

7. Continuous Feedback

Performance management at Innovfix is not limited to formal review periods. Managers are expected to conduct weekly reviews with each team member to assess progress against KPIs, provide feedback, and address any blockers or concerns.

As part of the weekly review, managers will assign a weekly rating to each employee based on their performance and output during that week. These weekly ratings are tracked and taken into consideration during the formal mid-year and annual review cycles.

Employees are expected to come prepared for their weekly reviews, with a clear update on their KPI progress, tasks completed, and any challenges faced during the week.

8. Appraisal Conversation

At the conclusion of each review cycle, every employee will have a formal one-on-one appraisal conversation with their manager. This is a structured two-way dialogue covering:

  • Performance feedback based on KPI achievement
  • Key achievements and contributions during the cycle
  • Areas requiring improvement and development
  • KPI setting for the upcoming cycle
  • Career growth discussions and aspirations
Appraisal conversations must be conducted respectfully, constructively, and privately. HR may be present if required.

9. Increment & Promotion Policy

Salary increments and promotions at Innovfix are entirely performance-based. There are no automatic or across-the-board increments or promotions. All decisions are made exclusively by Senior Management and higher authorities based on the employee's performance rating, overall KPI achievement, contribution, and conduct.

Increment + Promotion

For employees who demonstrate the required capability and readiness — both an increment and a promotion are awarded simultaneously. Promotion is subject to demonstrated readiness, a recommendation from the reporting manager and department head, and final approval from Senior Management.

Increment Only

For employees who are performing but not yet ready for the next level — an increment alone is awarded based on their performance during the cycle.

Exceptional Performers (6-Month Review)

Employees who qualify for a mid-year review based on exceptional performance may be considered for an early increment or promotion at the discretion of Senior Management and higher authorities.

All decisions are communicated to the employee by HR at the completion of their individual review cycle and are final.

10. Rewards & Recognition

Innovfix believes in acknowledging exceptional work beyond the formal increment cycle.

Employee of the Quarter

Every quarter, Innovfix formally recognises outstanding performers through the Employee of the Quarter award — open to all employees across all departments. The selected employee will be celebrated at a quarterly recognition event and will receive:

  • A token of appreciation as a gesture of recognition for their outstanding contribution
  • A formal appreciation communicated across the organisation
Selection is based on performance, contribution, and conduct during the quarter, as assessed and decided by Senior Management and higher authorities. Recognition is encouraged at all levels and is not limited to formal review periods.

11. Responsibilities

Employees
  • Understand and deliver on their assigned KPIs
  • Take ownership of their work and performance
  • Engage openly and honestly in appraisal conversations
  • Continuously seek feedback and work on areas of improvement
Managers
  • Set clear and measurable KPIs for each team member
  • Conduct weekly reviews and assign weekly ratings consistently
  • Complete all appraisal evaluations within the timelines set by HR
  • Recommend ratings that are fair, justified, and well-documented
  • Identify and escalate performance concerns to HR early
HR
  • Design and administer the performance review framework
  • Track and communicate individual employee review schedules
  • Ensure the process is consistent, fair, and timely across all departments
  • Support managers and employees through the appraisal process
  • Maintain all performance records securely and confidentially
  • Coordinate increment and promotion decisions with Senior Management
Senior Management & Higher Authorities
  • Review and approve all performance ratings
  • Make final decisions on increments and promotions
  • Ensure the overall integrity and fairness of the performance management process
  • Set the increment budget and guidelines for each review cycle

12. Confidentiality

All performance ratings, appraisal feedback, increment details, and promotion decisions are strictly confidential. Employees must not share or discuss their ratings or increments with colleagues.

Any breach of confidentiality will be treated as a disciplinary matter.

13. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR or earlier if organisational needs change. Any updates will be communicated to all employees in a timely manner.

Probation & Exit

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR

All Employees

1. Purpose

This policy outlines the probation review process at Innovfix Private Limited and defines the steps to be followed in the event of performance concerns, extension, or termination during probation. It ensures that every probation outcome is handled fairly, consistently, and with full transparency.

2. Probation Period

The standard probation period at Innovfix is 1 month from the date of joining. During this period, the employee's performance, conduct, and overall fit are assessed against the expectations communicated at the time of joining.

3. Expectations at Joining

At the time of joining, the manager communicates the role's KPIs and behavioural expectations to the probationer within the first week. These KPIs follow the same KPI-based framework as the Performance Management Policy and form the primary basis of the probation review.

Step 1 — Early Identification

The manager identifies any performance or behavioural concern early during probation and flags it to HR immediately. The earlier the better — managers must not wait until the last week of probation to raise concerns.

Step 2 — Informal Conversation

The manager has a direct, honest conversation with the employee about the concerns. Within 24 hours of the discussion, the manager sends a written summary to the employee via email, copying HR. The employee is given a clear picture of what improvement is expected and by when.

Step 3 — Probation Review Meeting

A formal review is conducted no later than 14 days before the probation end date. HR, the manager, and the employee are all present. Performance against the employee's set KPIs and expectations is reviewed formally.

Step 4 — Decision by Management

Based on the review, Senior Management and higher authorities make one of three decisions:

  • Confirm — employee passes probation and is confirmed as a full-time employee
  • Extend Probation — employee is given additional time (typically 1–3 months) to demonstrate the required improvement, at the manager's discretion
  • Terminate — employee is exited during or at the end of probation
If an employee's probation is extended and they still do not meet the required standards by the end of the extension, the outcome is determined by the manager in consultation with HR and Senior Management, and may result in termination.

Step 5 — Termination Communication

If the decision is to terminate, the termination letter is drafted by HR and approved by Senior Management before issuance. HR communicates this to the employee in a private, respectful setting. The termination letter covers:

  • Effective date of termination
  • Reason stated professionally — not meeting probation standards
  • Last working day
  • Details of final settlement

Step 6 — Notice Period During Probation

Party Notice Period
Employee 1 month
Employer 1 month or pay in lieu of notice

Step 7 — Full & Final Settlement

Processed within 30 days of the last working day, covering:

  • Salary for days worked
  • Any reimbursements pending
  • No gratuity — not applicable during probation
  • PF settlement as applicable

Step 8 — Exit Formalities

  • Return of all company assets — laptop, access cards, and any other equipment
  • The IT department is responsible for revoking all system and tool access by EOD on the last working day
  • Exit interview conducted by HR — recommended for all probation exits
  • Employees are reminded that their confidentiality and NDA obligations, signed at the time of joining, continue to apply in full after the last working day

4. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR or earlier if organisational needs change.

HR Policy · 04

Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH)

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR
Legal Reference
POSH Act 2013

1. Purpose

Innovfix Private Limited is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and dignified work environment for all employees. This policy establishes a zero-tolerance approach to sexual harassment in the workplace, in compliance with the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, interns, contractors, vendors, clients, and visitors at any Innovfix workplace or work-related setting — including off-site events, travel, and digital communication channels.

3. What Constitutes Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome act or behaviour of a sexual nature, whether physical, verbal, or non-verbal. Examples include:

  • Unwelcome physical contact or advances
  • Demand or request for sexual favours
  • Making sexually coloured remarks, jokes, or innuendos
  • Showing pornography or sexually explicit material
  • Any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature
  • Creating a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment through such conduct

4. Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)

Innovfix has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) as required under the POSH Act. The ICC is responsible for receiving, investigating, and resolving all complaints of sexual harassment.

  • Presiding Officer — a senior female employee of Innovfix
  • Two or more employee members — preferably with legal or social work background
  • One external member — from an NGO or organisation working in the field of women's rights
ICC member details are maintained by HR and available to all employees on request. The ICC constitution is reviewed annually.

5. How to File a Complaint

Any employee who has experienced or witnessed sexual harassment may submit a written complaint to the ICC or to HR within 3 months of the incident. In exceptional circumstances, this period may be extended by the ICC.

Complaints may be submitted to HR via email or in person. HR will forward the complaint to the ICC immediately upon receipt.

6. Investigation Process

  • The ICC will acknowledge receipt of the complaint within 7 days
  • Conciliation may be offered before a formal inquiry, if the complainant requests it
  • A formal inquiry, if initiated, will be completed within 60 days
  • The ICC will submit its findings and recommendations to Senior Management within 10 days of completing the inquiry
  • Action on the recommendations will be taken within 60 days of receipt of the report

7. Consequences

If the ICC finds the complaint to be substantiated, disciplinary action will be taken against the respondent. Actions may include warning, suspension, demotion, or termination, depending on the severity of the conduct. Cases may also be referred to law enforcement authorities where applicable.

False complaints made maliciously will also be treated as a misconduct and may result in disciplinary action against the complainant.

8. Confidentiality

The identity of the complainant, respondent, witnesses, and all information related to the complaint will be kept strictly confidential throughout the process. Disclosure of any information in violation of this policy will be treated as a serious disciplinary offence.

9. Non-Retaliation

Innovfix strictly prohibits any form of retaliation against an employee who has filed a complaint, served as a witness, or participated in an inquiry in good faith. Any retaliation will be treated as a separate disciplinary offence.

10. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR. The ICC will hold at least one awareness session per year for all employees.

Code of Conduct

1. Purpose

This policy defines the standards of professional behaviour expected of every employee at Innovfix Private Limited. It is the foundation on which trust, collaboration, and a healthy work culture are built. Every employee is expected to read, understand, and uphold these standards.

2. Professional Behaviour

Employees are expected to conduct themselves with integrity, respect, and professionalism at all times — with colleagues, managers, clients, vendors, and all other stakeholders.

  • Treat everyone with dignity and respect regardless of role, gender, background, or belief
  • Communicate honestly and constructively — in person and in writing
  • Raise disagreements through appropriate channels, not through gossip or passive conduct
  • Take ownership of mistakes and work to correct them

3. Attendance & Punctuality

Employees are expected to be present and reachable during core business hours and to self-report attendance accurately on Tessa. Refer to the Attendance & Working Hours Policy for full details.

4. Confidentiality

Employees must not share, disclose, or misuse any confidential information belonging to Innovfix — including product plans, financials, user data, business strategies, or personnel matters. This obligation continues after employment ends, as governed by the NDA signed at joining.

5. Use of Company Resources

Company-provided devices, tools, software, and office resources are for professional use. Employees must:

  • Use company equipment responsibly and return all assets upon exit
  • Not install unauthorised software on company devices
  • Not use company resources for personal business or side projects
  • Report lost or damaged equipment to IT and HR immediately

6. Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict — or appear to conflict — with the interests of Innovfix. Refer to the Moonlighting & Conflict of Interest Policy for full details.

7. Workplace Safety & Environment

Every employee is responsible for maintaining a safe, clean, and professional office environment. Behaviour that is threatening, violent, or creates an unsafe environment will not be tolerated and will result in immediate disciplinary action.

8. Social Media

Employees must not post content that defames the company, its leadership, products, or colleagues. Refer to the Social Media Policy for full details.

9. Disciplinary Action

Violations of this Code of Conduct will be taken seriously. Depending on the nature and severity of the misconduct, disciplinary action may include:

  • Verbal or written warning
  • Performance Improvement Plan
  • Suspension with or without pay
  • Termination of employment
  • Legal action where applicable
All disciplinary decisions are made by HR in consultation with Senior Management and are final.

10. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR or earlier if organisational needs change.

HR Policy · 07

Grievance Redressal

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR
Applies To
All Employees

1. Purpose

Innovfix Private Limited is committed to ensuring that every employee has a safe and effective way to raise workplace concerns. This policy provides a formal, fair, and confidential process for employees to voice grievances and have them addressed promptly.

2. What Constitutes a Grievance

A grievance is any genuine concern related to an employee's work, working conditions, or treatment at the workplace — including but not limited to:

  • Unfair treatment or perceived bias by a manager or colleague
  • Concerns about work conditions, workload, or safety
  • Disagreements related to leave, attendance records, or appraisal
  • Concerns about policy application or compliance
Grievances related to sexual harassment are handled exclusively under the POSH Policy and must be raised with the ICC.

3. Grievance Process

Step 1 — Raise with Manager

The employee raises the concern directly with their immediate manager. The manager must respond within 3 working days. If the concern involves the manager directly, the employee should proceed to Step 2.

Step 2 — Escalate to HR

If the issue is not resolved at Step 1, or involves the direct manager, the employee submits a written grievance to HR. HR will acknowledge within 2 working days and work towards resolution within 7 working days.

Step 3 — Escalate to Senior Management

If the issue remains unresolved after Step 2, HR escalates to Senior Management. A decision will be communicated to the employee within 14 working days of the original written grievance. This decision is final.

4. Confidentiality

All grievances and related discussions will be handled with strict confidentiality. Information will only be shared with those directly involved in resolving the matter.

5. Non-Retaliation

No employee will face any negative consequence for raising a grievance in good faith. Any form of retaliation against an employee who has raised a grievance will be treated as a serious disciplinary offence.

6. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR or earlier if organisational needs change.

Travel & Expense

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR
Applies To
Full-Time Employees

1. Purpose

This policy defines the travel allowance entitlement and expense reimbursement process for full-time employees at Innovfix Private Limited who commute to the office or travel for work-related purposes.

2. Eligibility

All full-time employees of Innovfix are eligible for travel reimbursement under this policy. Interns, contractors, and part-time staff are not covered unless specifically communicated at the time of joining.

3. Travel Allowance

Eligible employees are entitled to a monthly travel reimbursement of up to ₹3,000 for commute and work-related local travel. This applies to days the employee works from office (WFO).

Travel allowance is not applicable on WFH days or leave days. The ₹3,000 limit is the maximum claimable amount per month — actual reimbursement is based on bills submitted.

4. Reimbursement Process

  • Employees must upload valid bills or receipts on Tessa to claim reimbursement
  • Claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date — claims submitted later will not be processed
  • The manager reviews and approves reimbursement requests on Tessa
  • Approved claims are processed with the next payroll cycle

5. What is Reimbursable

  • Auto, cab, or bus fare for commuting to and from the office on WFO days
  • Local travel for official meetings or work assignments

6. What is Not Reimbursable

  • Travel on WFH days or days of leave
  • Personal travel unrelated to work
  • Claims submitted without valid bills or receipts
  • Claims exceeding the ₹3,000 monthly cap

7. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR. The travel allowance limit may be revised based on organisational requirements.

IT & Data Security

Effective Date
July 17, 2026
Version
1.0
Policy Owner
HR & IT
Applies To
All Employees

1. Purpose

This policy establishes the standards for responsible use of Innovfix's IT systems, devices, and data. It protects the company, its users, and its employees from security risks, data breaches, and misuse of technology resources.

2. Device Usage

  • Company-provided devices must be used primarily for work purposes
  • Employees must not share their device or login credentials with any other person
  • Devices must be locked when unattended
  • Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT and HR immediately
  • Employees must not install unauthorised software or applications on company devices

3. Password & Access Management

  • Employees must use strong passwords for all work accounts and change them regularly
  • Passwords must never be shared with colleagues, managers, or anyone else
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be enabled wherever available
  • Access to systems and tools is granted on a need-to-know basis and must not be shared

4. Data Handling & Confidentiality

Employees who have access to company data — including user data, financial records, product code, or business information — must handle it with the highest level of care.

  • Company data must not be stored on personal devices or shared via personal accounts

This policy outlines Innovfix Private Limited's maternity and paternity leave entitlements. Maternity leave provisions comply fully with the Maternity Benefit Act, 2017. Paternity leave is offered by Innovfix as a voluntary benefit in recognition of shared family responsibilities.

2. Eligibility

To be eligible for maternity leave, an employee must have worked at Innovfix for a minimum of 80 days in the 12 months preceding the expected date of delivery. Employees who do not meet this threshold are not entitled to paid maternity leave under this policy.

Paternity leave is available to all confirmed male employees with no minimum service requirement.

3. Maternity Leave Entitlement

Situation Leave Duration Pay
First & second child 26 weeks Full pay
Third child onwards 12 weeks Full pay
Adoption (child under 3 months) 12 weeks Full pay
Surrogacy (commissioning mother) 12 weeks Full pay
Miscarriage / medical termination 6 weeks Full pay
Maternity leave may commence up to 8 weeks before the expected delivery date. The remaining weeks are available post-delivery.

4. Paternity Leave Entitlement

All confirmed male employees are entitled to 10 days of paid paternity leave on the birth or adoption of a child. This leave must be availed within 3 months of the child's birth or adoption date. Paternity leave cannot be carried forward or encashed.

5. Required Documentation

Leave will not be processed without valid supporting documents. The following must be submitted to HR at each stage:

Stage Document Required
Before leave begins Medical certificate from a registered doctor confirming pregnancy and expected delivery date
After delivery Birth certificate or hospital discharge summary
Miscarriage / termination Medical certificate from a registered practitioner
Adoption Legal adoption order or official documentation of adoption
Paternity leave Birth certificate or hospital document confirming birth / adoption
Submission of false documentation will be treated as gross misconduct and grounds for immediate termination.

6. Salary Payment During Maternity Leave

Maternity leave pay is disbursed in two parts:

  • First instalment — paid before the leave begins, upon submission of the pre-leave medical certificate
  • Second instalment — paid after delivery, upon submission of the birth certificate or hospital discharge summary

7. Return to Work

HR will initiate a check-in with the employee at least 2 weeks before the expected return date to confirm the return plan. The employee must provide written confirmation of their return date.

Salary & Payroll

1. Purpose

This policy outlines how salaries are processed, when they are credited, what deductions apply, and how employees can access their payslips at Innovfix Private Limited. It is intended to provide full transparency on payroll and eliminate ambiguity around salary-related matters.

2. Salary Credit Date

Salaries are credited on the 30th or 31st of every month — the last day of the calendar month. In months with fewer days (e.g. February), salary is credited on the last working day of that month.

If the 30th or 31st falls on a bank holiday or weekend, salary will be credited on the preceding working day.

3. Salary Structure

Each employee's salary structure — including Basic Pay, HRA, and any applicable allowances — is defined in their offer letter. The offer letter is the authoritative document for the employee's compensation breakdown. Employees are encouraged to read and retain their offer letter for reference.

4. Statutory Deductions

The following statutory deductions are applied to salaries as required by Indian law. All applicable deductions are reflected in the employee's offer letter and monthly payslip.

Deduction Employee Contribution Employer Contribution Applicability
Provident Fund (PF) 12% of Basic Pay 12% of Basic Pay All eligible employees
ESI (Employee State Insurance) 0.75% of Gross Salary 3.25% of Gross Salary Gross salary ≤ ₹21,000/month
TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) As per Income Tax slab — All employees as applicable
TDS is calculated based on the employee's declared investment proofs and applicable tax regime. Employees must submit their investment declarations to Finance at the beginning of each financial year and actual proofs before March 31st to avoid excess deduction.

5. Leave Without Pay (LWP)

If an employee is absent without an approved leave on Tessa, the days will be treated as Leave Without Pay (LWP). Salary deduction for LWP is calculated as:

LWP Deduction = (Gross Monthly Salary ÷ Number of days in the month) × Number of LWP days

LWP days are determined based on attendance records on Tessa. Employees are encouraged to ensure their attendance is always accurately self-reported to avoid incorrect deductions.

6. Payslip

Payslips are automatically generated and available on Tessa every month once salary is processed. Employees can access, download, and save their payslips at any time from their Tessa profile.

The payslip reflects the complete salary breakdown including gross pay, all deductions, and net pay credited. Employees are encouraged to review their payslip every month.

7. Salary During Special Circumstances

Probation Period

Salary during probation is the same as the confirmed salary as stated in the offer letter. There is no separate probation-period pay structure.

Maternity & Paternity Leave

Full salary is paid during approved maternity and paternity leave as per the Maternity & Paternity Leave Policy.

Notice Period

Salary continues to be paid normally during the notice period. Any notice period shortfall or buyout is adjusted in the full and final settlement.

8. Salary Revision

Salary revisions at Innovfix are entirely performance-based and are processed through the annual performance review cycle. There are no automatic or across-the-board increments. Refer to the Performance Management Policy for details.

All revised salary structures will be reflected in a revised offer letter issued by HR at the time of the increment.

9. Salary Queries & Disputes

If an employee believes there is an error in their salary or payslip, they must:

  • Review their payslip on Tessa first to confirm the discrepancy
  • Raise the concern with HR within 7 days of salary credit with specific details of the discrepancy
  • HR will review with Finance and respond within 5 working days
  • If confirmed as an error, the correction will be processed in the next payroll cycle or sooner if the amount is significant
Salary queries raised after 30 days of the credit date may not be eligible for correction in the same financial year.

10. Confidentiality

Salary details are strictly confidential. Employees must not share or discuss their compensation with colleagues. Any breach of salary confidentiality will be treated as a disciplinary matter.

11. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR and Finance or earlier if statutory rates or organisational requirements change.

Employees may raise a concern under this policy if they have a genuine, reasonable belief that any of the following has occurred or is likely to occur:

  • Financial fraud, embezzlement, or misuse of company funds
  • Corruption, bribery, or dishonest conduct
  • Serious violation of company policies or applicable law
  • Falsification of records, data, or documentation
  • Actions that endanger the health, safety, or welfare of employees
  • Deliberate concealment of any of the above
This policy is not intended for personal grievances or interpersonal disputes — those must be raised through the Grievance Redressal Policy. Harassment concerns must be raised through the POSH Policy.

3. How to Report

Employees may raise a whistleblower concern by submitting a written report to HR. If the concern involves HR, it may be escalated directly to Senior Management.

  • HR will acknowledge receipt of the report within 5 working days
  • An investigation will be initiated promptly and conducted confidentially
  • The investigation will be handled by HR and Senior Management — or an independent party if the concern involves senior personnel
  • The outcome will be communicated to the reporting employee (where identity is known) within a reasonable timeframe

5. Confidentiality

The identity of the reporting employee will be protected to the fullest extent possible throughout the investigation. Information shared under this policy will only be disclosed to those directly involved in resolving the matter.

6. Non-Retaliation

Innovfix strictly prohibits any form of retaliation against an employee who raises a concern in good faith under this policy — including dismissal, demotion, harassment, or any other form of unfair treatment. Any act of retaliation will itself be treated as a serious disciplinary offence.

Reporting in good faith is protected even if the concern turns out to be unsubstantiated, as long as the report was not made maliciously or with intent to cause harm.

7. False or Malicious Reports

Reports made knowingly with false information or with malicious intent to harm a colleague will be treated as gross misconduct and may result in disciplinary action including termination.
